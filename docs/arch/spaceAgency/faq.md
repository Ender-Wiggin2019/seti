[size=24]GENERAL QUESTIONS[/size]

[heading]🛰️Can we play 5 rounds with organizations?[/heading]
[color=#006d8f]We do not recommend that. Organizations were balanced for 4 rounds, and so if you would play one more round, some of them could be more powerful. Also, 5 rounds with the extra stuff added by the organizations would lead to a significantly longer game. In fact, be prepared that the length of a 4-round game with organizations doesn’t differ that much from the base game.
[/color]
[heading]🛰️Can we play 4 rounds with quick start cards but without organizations?[/heading]
[color=#006d8f]The quick start cards don’t fully compensate for the missing round – part of the compensation comes from the extra resources and abilities of the organizations. So without organizations, you will achieve less than intended. It also impacts the game balance. But if you want to give it a go, use the starting income card from the base game in place of an organization.
[/color]
[heading]🛰️Do organizations replace the starting income cards from the base game?[/heading]
[color=#006d8f]Yes. When you decide to play with organizations, leave the starting income cards in the box.
[/color]
[heading]🛰️Starting income increases on organizations[/heading]
[color=#006d8f]Most organizations allow you to perform multiple income increases at the start of the game. When choosing which card to tuck, you always have at least four options, plus any additional cards gained from quick start cards or your organization’s starting resources.

Since you often tuck multiple cards, you can first tuck a card that provides a random card as income, draw that card, and then decide whether to use it for the next tuck.

Most players prefer to resolve income increases simultaneously. However, in a highly competitive occasion, you can agree to resolve them in player order: first, everyone resolves their quick start cards and organization starting resources, then the first player resolves all their income increases, followed by the second player, and so on.
[/color]
[size=24]INDIVIDUAL ORGANIZATIONS[/size]

[heading]📡 #1 ORBITAL DYNAMICS[/heading]
[color=#db2489] [b]Once per round ability: [/b]If one of the two probes you decided to move was on a space with asteroids, you can add another move to be able to get out of it (e.g. by paying 1 energy or by discarding a card with 1 move in its free-action corner).
[/color]
[heading]📡 #2 FENWICK RESEARCH CENTER[/heading]
[color=#db2489] [b]Passive power:[/b] The 5 publicity cost is mandatory – you can’t decide to develop a technology for the usual 6 publicity. While very rare, this may be important with some of the new cards.

[b]Once per round ability:[/b] The card you gain can come either from the card row or it can be a random card from the main deck. When choosing to take a card from the deck, you must reveal it so everyone can check its free-action corner effect. Note that taking a random card can leave you with a free-action corner effect you might not be able to use (like 1 move, for example).
[/color]
[heading]📡 #3 STRATUS CORE[/heading]
[color=#db2489] [b]Once per round ability:[/b] Don’t forget that you do not discard the cards in the card row.
[/color]
[heading]📡 #4 DEEP SKY SURVEY[/heading]
[color=#db2489] [b]Passive power:[/b] The no energy for analyzing data is mandatory – you can’t decide to use the action for the usual 1 energy. While very rare, this may be important with some of the new cards.

[b]Once per round ability: [/b]You can theoretically exchange an alien card with a card from the card row. The alien card would then get in the card row.
[/color]
[heading]📡 #5 TURING SYSTEMS[/heading]
[color=#db2489] [b]Once per round ability: [/b]Choosing the tech doesn’t apply as researching it – for example, it doesn’t allow you to mark a trigger that requires researching a tech. It only allows you to use the tech’s ability for one turn.

If you choose the first orange tech that increases your probe limit, you can launch a probe that turn even if it exceeds your usual limit. You don’t lose the probe when the tech’s effect ends at the end of the turn. However, for subsequent rounds, your original limit applies, so you may need to get rid of some probes before launching more.
[/color]
[heading]📡 #6 COSMOS STRATEGY GROUP[/heading]
[color=#db2489] [b]Passive power:[/b] It allows you to mark spaces and gain the depicted rewards whenever you play a card (for its main action) with the corresponding sector color shown in its top right corner. When you play a card with a black sector color, you can choose which space to mark.

Although the passive power resembles a triggerable mission, it does not count as such.

[b]Once per round ability:[/b] It not only gives you a card but also clears all marked spaces on the passive power, allowing them to be marked again.
[/color]
[heading]📡 #7 SENTINEL PROBE NETWORK[/heading]
[color=#db2489] [b]Once per round ability:[/b] When you play a card as a main action, you can use this ability to also gain that card’s free-action corner effect. You first need to fully resolve the card and only then gain its free-action corner effect.

Note that the ability can’t be used on an alien card.
[/color]
[heading]📡 #8 MISSION RELAY[/heading]
[color=#db2489] You start the game with a specific gold scoring tile already marked. The picture on the organization card shows the two sides of the same tile, so depending on which side is up in that particular game, you mark the lowest-scoring space on that side. This counts as extra to the usual three markings you can do per game. But of course, since this one tile is already marked by you, you can’t mark it again.

There is an important interaction with the Solvay Conference card (#113). It’s likely that you’ll mark all four gold scoring tiles with this organization – in that case, the gold box on the Solvay Conference has no effect for you.

[b]Once per round ability:[/b] The card you gain can come either from the card row or it can be a random card from the main deck. When choosing to take a card from the deck, you must reveal it so everyone can check its income corner.
[/color]
[heading]📡 #9 XENOLAB[/heading]
[color=#db2489] This organization uses three special tiles. Each tile shows a specific discounted main action on its active side and a particular trace on its reverse side. All three tiles begin the game active side up.

When you use a tile’s discounted action, flip that tile to its reverse side – it cannot be used again until it is flipped back to its active side. To flip a tile back to its active side, you must gain the specific trace shown on its reverse side. Don't worry, you are still free to mark that trace – you do not "pay" the trace for flipping the tile.
[/color]
[heading]📡 #10 FUTURESPAN INSTITUTE[/heading]
[color=#db2489] This organization starts with a special Futurespan token, which begins in your possession and can be spent to use the top ability. This ability lets you plan cards ahead and eventually play them for free.

[b]Using the ability:[/b]
  1) Place a card from your hand face up beside the organization card.
  2) Place the Futurespan token on the scoring track ahead of your current score. The card’s cost determines how many spaces ahead of your score counter the token should be placed.
  3) When your score counter catches up to the Futurespan token, you may play the pre-planned card as a main action for free. You do not have to play it immediately – as long as your score counter is on or ahead of the token, you may play the card. When you do, take the Futurespan token back from the scoring track, allowing you to prepare another card.

Important: You can only have one card pre-planned at a time. You must reach the Futurespan token and play the previously planned card before preparing another.

Once a card is prepared, it is no longer considered part of your hand. It does not count toward your hand limit, and you cannot discard it, use it for a free-action effect, or tuck it for income.

You can't use the ability to prepare Centaurians cards since those cards cost energy to play.

[b]Once per round ability: [/b]To use this ability, the Futurespan token must be on the scoring track. By advancing the token, you are delaying the moment when you can play your prepared card for free.
[/color]
[heading]📡 #11 HELION ASSEMBLY[/heading]
[color=#db2489] [b]Passive power:[/b] It allows you to mark spaces and gain the depicted rewards whenever you research a corresponding tech.

Although the passive power resembles a triggerable mission, it does not count as one. If researching a tech would match both any of the available spaces on the passive power and a space on a triggerable mission, you can mark both.

[b]Once per round ability: [/b]To gain the income increase, you must “turn off” one of your probe or telescope techs. Do this by removing the tech from its slot and placing it beside your player board. You may no longer use that tech’s ability. Even though that tech’s slot on your player board is now empty, you are not allowed to research that same tech again. For all other purposes (e.g., end of game scoring), treat that tech as if you had researched it.

The once per round ability also clears all marked spaces on the passive power, allowing them to be marked again.
[/color][/q]

[q="CzechGamesEdition"][size=24]SETI: SPACE AGENCIES – CARDS[/size]

[heading]🚀 #2 Tracking and Data Relay Satellite[/heading]
[color=#a96800] The free-action corner effect refers to the card’s top left corner.

Some alien cards give 2 publicity, 1 data + 1 point or 1 move + 1 point in their free-action corner. All those qualify for this card’s effect.
In the case of the 2 publicity free-action corner, you gain only 1 publicity for showing such a card.
[/color]
[heading]🚀 #5 Servicing Mission[/heading]
[color=#a96800] You need to resolve the movement before checking whether you have a probe on a space  adjacent to Earth.
[/color]
[heading]🚀 #7 Twin Probes[/heading]
[color=#a96800] The card’s mission requires you to have 2 probes in space at once (not turned into a lander, nor an orbiter), each of them on a space with a different planet (Earth does not count in this case).
[/color]
[heading]🚀 #8 Quantum Data Storage[/heading]
[color=#a96800] A data pool is the space in the left part of your player board that can hold up to 6 data. Data placed in your computer do not count for this effect.
[/color]
[heading]🚀 #10 First Human in Space[/heading]
[color=#a96800] Note that the publicity track goes only up to 10. So if you do not have 0 publicity when playing this card, all the excess publicity that goes over 10 will be lost.
[/color]
[heading]🚀 #12 Two-planet Flyby Maneuver[/heading]
[color=#a96800] “Visiting a planet” means that you need to move to a space with the planet. Simply being on a planet when you play this card does not count – you would need to leave that space and then return to it, and that would count only as one of the two different planets.

The effect lasts for the whole turn, so you can gain the benefit also with the additional moves
performed as free actions after evaluating this card.
[/color]
[heading]🚀 #13 James Clerk Maxwell Telescope[/heading]
[color=#a96800] You do not pay the base cost of the Scan action but you still need to pay the additional costs of your purple techs if you decide to use them.

Note that you gain the signal token only after resolving the scan action, so the token cannot be used during that same scan action.
[/color]
[heading]🚀 #15 Iterative Engineering[/heading]
[color=#a96800] For example, if the tech tile has the [1 energy] immediate bonus printed on it, you gain it once for acquiring the tile and then again thanks to the card’s effect.

The card’s effect doesn’t apply to the extra immediate bonuses that two of the tech tiles grant: [probe launch] and [2 data].
[/color]
[heading]🚀 #16 Astronaut Training Experience[/heading]
[color=#a96800] Note that you first need to resolve the card’s main effect before you can check the mission condition. So if you would have 0 publicity when playing the card, you won’t be able to complete its mission right away, because you first gain the 4 publicity from the main effect.
[/color]
[heading]🚀 #17 Paid Media Coverage[/heading]
[color=#a96800] You can theoretically play the card even for 0 credits. You won’t gain anything from it, so it could only make sense for timing reasons.

Note that the publicity track only goes up to 10. While you can spend more than 5 credits when playing this card, any excess publicity will be lost. However, you will still earn 2 points for each additional credit you spend.
[/color]
[heading]🚀 #18 Contracted Research[/heading]
[color=#a96800] You can play this card even when you have 0 publicity and still gain the tech. That is actually the most effective way to do it.
[/color]
[heading]🚀 #19 New Assignment[/heading]
[color=#a96800] When you remove the orbiter from the 1st space (with the extra 3-points reward) that space becomes available again. This applies also when there are multiple orbiters around that planet’s orbit – they do not slide when the 1st space gets empty, so the next time someone orbits the planet, they gain those 3 points.

Placing a probe on the planet doesn't count as a launch. Neither does it count as a visit, which means you do not gain the publicity for visiting that planet either.

The card allows you to exceed your limit of probes in space. But the limit still applies for your regular launches so to launch another probe you will probably need to get rid of those you have in space first (usually turning them into an orbiter or lander).
[/color]

Orbiters and landers are not considered “probes in space”.

[color=#a96800] You can decide not to discard any card.
[/color]
[heading]🚀 #29 Abandoned Mission[/heading]
[color=#a96800] You may return a conditional mission that you have not yet completed (even if its condition has already been met), or a triggerable mission that still has at least one unmarked space.

If you return a triggerable mission with any marked spaces, return those markers to your supply.

[heading]🚀 #32 MUREP Idea Competition[/heading]
[color=#a96800] You still gain 1 publicity and 2 random cards, even when you have no cards left in hand after playing this card.

[heading]🚀 #33 Delayed Launch[/heading]
[color=#a96800] Remember, you can’t take any free actions after you pass. So if you launch a probe while triggering this card’s mission, you won’t be able to move it until the next round.

When you’re already at your probe limit when you pass, you can’t perform the launch from the mission. However, you can still mark the mission, just without applying the launch effect.
[/color]
[heading]🚀 #34 Private Sector Investment[/heading]
[color=#a96800] The first effect allows you to increase your income. To do this, tuck another card from your hand for income and immediately gain its income resource.

For the second effect, determine which tech type you have the most of (probe, telescope, or computer techs). Then draw 1 random card for each tech tile of that color.
[/color]
[heading]🚀 #35 NASA Exoplanet Archive[/heading]

[/color]
[heading]🚀 #37 Pandora Satellite[/heading]

You mark the signals by replacing data in the sectors with your markers as usual, but instead of gaining those data, return them to the general supply.

There are 8 sectors. In each sector, you must have either won it or have at least one signal marked in it.

[heading]🚀 #ET.1 [/heading]
[color=#a96800] There are 7 types (colors) of glyph tokens. The gold box gives you 3 points for each token of the type you have the most of. Those tokens still count towards the sets of different tokens that you score at the end of the game.
[/color]
[heading]🚀 #ET.3 [/heading]
[color=#a96800] Unfortunately, there was an error in the first print run of this card. The triggerable mission's conditions should use a slightly different icon – hexagonal instead of rounded, to make sure it refers to performing an orbit/land action and not simply having an orbiter/lander as the rounded icon might suggest. In other words, you can mark one of this mission's triggers when you orbit or land.
[/color]
[heading]🚀 #ET.9 [/heading]
[color=#a96800] There are 7 types (colors) of glyph tokens. The gold box gives you 1 point for each glyph in your largest set of different tokens. So you can gain up to 7 points this way, in case you have at least one complete set containing all 7 glyph types. The set still scores at the end of the game as usual.
[/color]
[heading]🚀 #ET.11 Biosignature Screening / #ET.12 Physical Characterization / #ET.13 Genome Characterization[/heading]
[color=#a96800] Marked overflow spaces below the Amoeba board also apply for this card’s gold box.
[/color]
[heading]🚀 #ET.14 Breakthrough Theory[/heading]
[color=#a96800] Marked overflow spaces below the Amoeba board also count towards the mission condition. Only the three discovery spaces that need to be marked to discover the species do not apply.

The reward for completing the mission gives you points for empty spaces on the Amoeba board. That means that the overflow spaces below the board do not count.
In a rare case, due to the card #ET.19 Extreme Conditions Testing, a discovery space below the Amoeba board can be also empty. It doesn’t count towards the reward either because it is not part of the Amoeba board.
[/color]
[heading]🚀 #ET.19 Extreme Conditions Testing[/heading]
[color=#a96800] You can remove your marker either from a space on the Amoeba board, or from a discovery space below it, or even from an overflow space belonging to this species. Based on the color of that space, gain the bonuses from all organelle tokens in the matching section, and move the tokens as usual.
[/color]
[heading]🚀 #ET.34 / #ET.38[/heading]
[color=#a96800] The effect allows you to exceed your limit of probes in space. But the limit still applies for your regular launches so to launch another probe you will probably need to get rid of those you have in space first (usually turning them into an orbiter or lander).
[/color]

[heading]🚀 #SE EN 03 - Space Agencies[/heading]
[color=#a96800] After playing the card, launch a probe and gain a signal token. Plus you gain a small advantage in your next game when you play with organizations. Normally you are dealt 3 quick start cards at random and you choose 2. Thanks to this card’s effect you are dealt one extra card, but you still choose only 2.

This is an unusual effect because it transfers to the next game. You need to remember that you played this card previously. Not everyone likes these kinds of effects so be prepared that the other players may have some objections (especially if they were not part of that last game). But remember that it is in fact only a very small advantage, and that the main purpose of this card was to make you remember your last game and have some fun. Don’t take it too seriously.
