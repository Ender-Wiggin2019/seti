# SETI Rules (Reformatted)

> This version reorganizes the original text into a practical handbook for faster learning and in-game lookup.

## 1) Game Objective

- The game lasts **5 rounds**.
- Players score VP mainly through:
  - planet orbiting/landing
  - signals and sector competition
  - life traces and alien discovery
  - technologies and card effects
  - end-game scoring cards and gold scoring tiles
- After round 5, highest VP wins.
- **No tiebreaker.**

## 2) Setup

### 2.1 Shared Board Setup

1. Build the main board (planetary board + solar system board + tech board).
2. Shuffle 5 alien species boards; randomly place 2 face down.
3. Randomize the 3 central discs and 4 surrounding sector boards:
   - must form 8 valid sectors
   - each sector has 1 nearby star
   - sun in the center
4. Shuffle main deck; reveal 3 cards as card row.
5. Put energy and credits nearby.
6. Fill each nearby star's data slot with data tokens.
7. Place gold scoring tiles nearby, random side face up.
8. Neutral milestones at 20/30 VP:
   - 1p/2p: 2 neutral markers on each
   - 3p: 1 neutral marker on each
   - 4p: ignore neutral milestones
9. Tech setup:
   - 12 tech types (4 per color, 3 colors)
   - shuffle each tech's 4 matching tiles into a stack
   - tech side face down
   - place one 2 VP tile on top of each stack
10. Prepare 4 end-of-round card stacks:
    - each stack = players + 1 cards
    - after passing in rounds 1-4, player takes 1 card from current stack
11. Rotation reminders:
    - 1 token on marked space of tech board
    - 1 token on round-1 end-of-round stack

### 2.2 Player Setup

1. Choose color and take all matching pieces.
2. Put publicity marker at 4 on publicity track.
3. Determine first player (last one who saw a UFO):
   - give first player marker
   - place score markers: P1 at 1, P2 at 2, P3 at 3, P4 at 4
4. Take starting income card and starting resources:
   - 4 publicity
   - 4 credits
   - 3 energy
   - 5 random cards
5. Tuck one card as income under starting income card.
6. Flip start card to base income side (used from round 2 onward).

## 3) Round Structure

### 3.1 Turn Basics

- On your turn: take **exactly 1 main action**.
- During your turn, you may take any number of legal free actions.
- After your turn, resolve:
  - milestones
  - discovered species (if any)
- Play proceeds clockwise, skipping passed players.

### 3.2 End of Round

- Round ends when all players have passed.
- Then:
  1. everyone gains income
  2. first-player marker passes left
  3. rotation reminder token moves to next round stack

## 4) Cards at a Glance

- A card can be used for **only one mode** at a time.
- If played as a main action, it cannot also be used as free-action discard.

Card zones:
- **Top-left**: free-action discard effect
- **Middle**: main action (pay cost shown on left-middle)
- **Bottom**: mission / end-game VP
- **Top-right**: sector color (for Scan)
- **Bottom-right**: income type when tucked

## 5) Main Actions

There are 8 main actions:
- Launch Probe
- Orbit
- Land
- Scan
- Analyze Data
- Play Card
- Research Tech
- Pass

### 5.1 Launch Probe

- Cost: 2 credits
- Place 1 probe on Earth.
- Normally each player can have only 1 probe "in space" (solar system rings).
- Probes on planetary board are **not** counted as "in space."
- Probes are moved via free-action movement.

### 5.2 Orbit a Planet

- Cost: 1 credit + 1 energy
- Requirement: your probe is on a non-Earth planet space.
- Convert probe into orbiter:
  - remove from solar system board
  - place in that planet's orbit on planetary board
- Gain shown orbit bonus (usually includes income increase).
- First player to orbit that planet gains 3 VP.
- No limit to orbiters per planet.

### 5.3 Land on Planet/Moon

- Cost: 3 energy (or 2 if any player's orbiter is already at that planet)
- Requirement: your probe on non-Earth planet space.
- Convert probe into lander on planetary board.
- Gain planet-center rewards (always includes life trace + VP).
- First lander on planet gains extra data (cover spot to mark taken).
- Mars has 2 first-lander spots.
- No limit to landers per planet.

Moons:
- You cannot land on moons unless allowed by effect/tech.
- Moon spaces allow only 1 lander each.

Life traces:
- On gaining a life-trace icon, place marker on matching discovery space if available.
- If both matching spaces are full and species not discovered, use overflow space.
- Claiming discovery space gives 1 publicity + 5 VP (plus any printed bonus).
- Universal trace icon is a wildcard for any trace color.

### 5.4 Scan Nearby Stars

- Cost: 1 credit + 2 energy
- Core source of red traces.
- Perform scan-board steps in chosen order:
  - mark 1 signal in Earth's sector
  - discard 1 card from card row and mark 1 signal in matching sector
- Telescope techs can add more signal marking (with costs if required).
- You always mark at least 2 signals; up to 4 with tech support.

Signal marking:
- Take leftmost data token from that sector's data slot.
- Put it into your data pool.
- Place your marker in the vacated slot.
- If placed in second data slot, score 2 VP immediately.
- You may place extra markers beyond slot capacity:
  - no data gained from those extras
  - extras still count for winning sector

Sector completion:
- If last data token is removed, sector is completed.
- Resolve completed sectors after your main action (any order).
- Most markers wins:
  - winner places marker by nearby star and gains shown reward
  - tie breaker: marker placed later wins
  - everyone who contributed at least 1 marker gains 1 publicity

Sector reset:
- Determine second place (same tiebreaker).
- Second place leaves a marker on first slot (if any).
- Return all other markers to players.
- Refill empty slots with data tokens.
- Sector can be won again later (with lower/overflow reward).

### 5.5 Analyze Data

- Cost: 1 energy
- Requirement: top row of computer is full.
  - bottom row spaces from tech may be full or empty
- Discard all data tokens in computer (not data pool).
- Mark one blue trace for either alien.
- Computer becomes empty and can be refilled.

### 5.6 Play a Card

- Pay card's main-action cost and resolve effect.
- If the effect contains other actions, those are performed without paying those action costs.
- After resolution:
  - normal cards -> discard pile
  - mission / end-game scoring cards -> keep in front of you

Mission cards:
- **Conditional mission**:
  - complete as free action when condition is met (during your turn)
  - if already met on play, may complete immediately
  - completion can be delayed
- **Trigger mission**:
  - only triggers from events after mission is played
  - each trigger circle can be covered once
  - may trigger outside your turn
  - if one effect triggers multiple mission rewards, choose one space at a time
  - once all circles are covered, mission auto-completes

End-game scoring cards:
- Cards with gold boxes score at game end.

### 5.7 Research a Tech

- Cost: 6 publicity
- First, rotate the solar system.
- Then choose one tech you do not have and take top tile from that stack.
- No duplicate tech allowed.

First-take bonus:
- First player taking from a stack discards that stack's 2 VP tile and scores 2 VP immediately.

Apply tech:
- Gain immediate printed bonus.
- Flip and place on your board.
- Probe (orange) and telescope (red) have fixed slots.
- Computer (blue) can go in any computer slot.
- If data token already there, slip tile under token.
- This does not count as "placing data" (no 2 VP placement trigger).

Tech from card effects:
- Works like regular research, including rotation.
- No publicity cost from card effect.
- If effect grants a tech you already have (or you already have all 4 of that type), ignore that part.

Solar system rotation details:
- Rotate whenever you research (main action or card effect).
- Also rotate once each round when first player passes.
- Yellow icon rotates top disc, orange icon middle disc, blue icon bottom disc.
- Probe figures rotate with discs and may be bumped to next space.
- This movement is free; publicity gained by movement is immediate.

### 5.8 Pass

- Pass is a main action, so free actions are still allowed first.
- Then resolve in order:
  1. discard down to 4 cards
  2. if first to pass this round, rotate solar system (round 5 still rotates; token is discarded)
  3. choose 1 card from current end-of-round stack, return/discard rest as instructed

Round ends when last player passes and takes one of the final two cards.

## 6) Free Actions

### 6.1 Movement

- Spend movement points to move probe to adjacent non-diagonal space.
- Leaving asteroids costs +1 extra movement point.
- Moving through publicity icon grants publicity even if you do not stop.

### 6.2 Place Data

- During your turn, move data from data pool to computer.
- Place left to right.
- On a space with an effect, resolve immediately.
- Data can only be placed during your turn.
- For 2-space tech columns: bottom space optional, but cannot fill before top.
- Data pool limit is 6. Excess gained data must be discarded.

### 6.3 Complete Mission

- Complete eligible mission during your turn as free action.

### 6.4 Use Free-Action Corner

- Discard a card from hand to resolve its top-left free action.

### 6.5 Buy Card

- Cost: 3 publicity
- Gain from card row or top of deck.
- Refill card row immediately after taking from row.
- If deck is empty, reshuffle discard pile.

### 6.6 Exchange Resources

- Trade 2 of one type (credits / energy / cards) for 1 of another type.
- Cards gained come from card row or top of deck.

## 7) Milestones

Milestones are resolved **between turns** after current player finishes free actions.

### 7.1 Gold Milestones

- Trigger when you reach/pass 25, 50, or 70 VP.
- Choose one gold scoring tile and mark one space.
- You cannot mark the same tile twice.
- First chooser gets highest-value space, next chooser gets next value, etc.

### 7.2 Neutral Milestones (non-4p only)

- At 20 and 30 VP, a neutral research group marks alien discovery spaces.
- Move neutral marker to leftmost empty space among 6 discovery spaces (3 per alien).
- Can cause alien discovery.
- If all 6 spaces already full, no effect.

### 7.3 Multiple Milestones

- If multiple players need resolution, resolve from player whose turn just ended, then clockwise.
- Neutral milestone always resolves last.

## 8) Discovering Alien Species

A species is discovered when all three discovery spaces below its board are filled (player/neutral markers).

When discovered (after turn ends):
1. Flip species board.
2. Read species-specific rule sheet.
3. Apply setup for revealed species and reward players who filled discovery spaces.

Notes:
- If milestones and discovery both trigger, resolve milestones first.
- Overflow spaces accept extra traces and still count for "marked trace" checks.
- If you must mark trace but matching normal spaces are full, place in overflow and score 3 VP.
- Alien species come with special card decks:
  - generally stronger than regular cards
  - can only be gained through species effects
  - follow normal hand rules, except Exertian cards do not count toward hand limit
- Extra spaces on alien boards work like discovery spaces for future trace rewards.
- Neutral markers only use the six discovery spaces below species boards.

## 9) Between Rounds Checklist

1. Everyone gains income from starting card + tucked income cards.
2. Pass first-player marker one seat left.
3. Move rotation reminder to next round card stack.

## 10) End Game Scoring

After round 5, when last player passes:
- score end-game scoring cards (gold-box cards)
- score gold scoring tiles
- apply any additional species-specific scoring steps

Highest VP wins.

## 11) Quick Rule Reminders

- One turn = one main action (+ optional free actions).
- A card cannot be both main-action use and free-action discard in the same use.
- Max 1 probe "in space" per player by default.
- Data pool max is 6.
- Marker placed later wins ties in sector majority.
- Rotation happens on every research and first pass each round.
- Missions that trigger from actions only see events after mission enters play.
- Milestones are resolved between turns, not immediately mid-action.
